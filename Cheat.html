1. üí™ What is a "Comp" (Composition)?

Comp = Troop distribution: Infantry / Range / Cavalry.

Always scaled to 20 points.

Examples:

  • 659 = 6 Inf / 5 Range / 9 Cav
  • 866 = 8 Inf / 6 Range / 6 Cav
  • 4412 = 4 Inf / 4 Range / 12 Cav

Rule:

  • Always add parts to 20.
  • If not ‚Üí double or normalize.

Cavalry Wedge Wide Cav spread

3. üî™ How to Instantly Read a Comp

  • First number = Infantry parts
  • Second number = Range parts
  • Third number = Cavalry parts

Examples:

  • 659 ‚Üí 6 Inf, 5 Range, 9 Cav
  • 866 ‚Üí 8 Inf, 6 Range, 6 Cav
Note: If comp looks weird (ex: 4-3-5)? Double it to normalize (becomes 8-6-10).

1. üéØ Wedge Formation Tactics

Range Wedge vs Range-heavy enemies (e.g., 7112):

  • Spreads Infantry wider
  • Makes Cavalry stay back longer
  • Result: Cavalry survives longer, joins battle later when Range squads are already softened
  • Range Wedge ruins 7112 comp mechanisms because Infantry takes longer to collapse

Infantry Wedge vs Cavalry-heavy enemies:

  • Wider Infantry spread stalls Cavalry charges longer
  • Gives Range squads more time to clean up

3. üîç Retargeting Mechanics

Retargeting in battle is a critical mechanic that decides who wins in mirror matchups.

Important: In many 4-2-4 vs 4-2-4 Range Phalanx battles, whoever gets their Range killed FIRST often gains battle dynamics in their favor.

When Range squads are eliminated:

  • The timing of Range destruction heavily influences battle outcome
  • Killing enemy Range faster can sometimes be a disservice
  • If both Range are killed simultaneously, expect a close match

3. üé≠ Formation Logic

Range Phalanx vs. Cavalry Phalanx:

Range troops attack enemy cavalry multiple times before the enemy infantry reaches the front line, decimating significant cavalry numbers early.

Infantry Phalanx vs. Cavalry Phalanx:

Enemy cavalry can only slip one attack with max strength before getting touched by your ranged units.

Range Phalanx vs. Infantry Phalanx:

Your cavalry reaches enemy infantry before they can engage your ranged units. Enemy infantry never attacks ranged with maximum strength.
